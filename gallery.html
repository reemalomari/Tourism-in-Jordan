Our Gallery

showing a few introductory pictures of the most prominent tourist areas in Jordan
with a simple overview of each of them.


Jerash is a wonderful blend of Greco-Roman and Oriental influences. A great place to visit during spring. Every year Jerash also hosts the Jerash Festival of Culture and Arts, a three-week summer program filled with folk dance, music and theater performances.

×

The picturesque natural wonders of the Dead Sea are ideal for religious tourism and enjoyment. The main attraction of the Dead Sea is the warm and salty water itself-ten times saltier than sea water, rich in salts of magnesium chloride, sodium, potassium and many other species.

×

Petra is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Inhabited by the Nabateans, Edomites, and Romans, Petra combined the knowledge and skill of these civilizations to create this universal wonder. Caravans laden with incense, silk, spices, and other exotic goods lay in Petra.

×

Wadi Rum is a famous Jordanian desert landscape and one of the best places to visit in the country. The beauty of this rugged landscape has made it the country's main movie star, playing leading roles in films as diverse as the award-winning Theeb in Jordan (where she played her).

×

The Roman Theater is a famous landmark in the Jordanian capital, with a capacity of six thousand seats. Due to the quality of its sound system, it is used in national concerts and annual country festivals.

×

Ma'in is a wonderful hot spring waterfall resort located by the Dead Sea. This resort is located at a depth of 264 meters below sea level amidst a peaceful landscape.
